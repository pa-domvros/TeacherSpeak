TeacherSpeak

Effective Teacher-Student Communication

Created by Panagiotis Domvros, English and Drama Teacher

Building Trust & Connection

  • "Tell me more about your idea."

    This shows interest in a student's thoughts and encourages them to elaborate.

  • "I appreciate your honesty..."

    This encourages truth-telling and builds a foundation of trust.

  • "You're really good at..."

    This builds connection through sincere, specific praise.

  • "Thank you for trusting me..."

    This affirms a student's vulnerability and builds a trusting relationship.

  • "I can see this project means a lot..."

    This recognizes a student's emotional investment and effort.

Fostering Collaboration & Problem-Solving

  • "What kind of help would be most useful..."

    This offers support without making assumptions about a student's needs.

  • "Let's work together to find a solution..."

    This shifts the dynamic to a collaborative effort.

  • "Help me understand what part is most confusing..."

    This helps to dig beneath a student's frustration to find the root of the problem.

  • "What's the best way for me to help..."

    This empowers students to take ownership of their learning.

  • "I don't want to assume..."

    This avoids misjudgment and invites clarity about a student's work.

  • "How can we make this concept easier..."

    This shifts the focus to collaborative problem-solving for a student's learning.

Promoting Empathy & Respect

  • "That sounds like it was a really tough problem..."

    This validates a student's struggles and builds empathy.

  • "I hear what you're saying..."

    This acknowledges a student's perspective without immediately agreeing or disagreeing.

  • "How would you feel if..."

    This promotes empathy and helps students see things from another's point of view.

  • "I respect your point of view..."

    This maintains dignity and models respectful disagreement.

  • "That's not a fair consequence."

    This acknowledges an injustice or imbalance directly.

  • "That makes total sense."

    This validates a student's reasoning or feelings without necessarily agreeing with their conclusion.

  • "Would you like advice, or just for me to listen?"

    This clarifies a student's needs and respects their emotional space.

Modeling Accountability & Humility

  • "I've never thought about it that way before..."

    This signals openness to new ideas and models intellectual curiosity.

  • "Let me think about that..."

    This sets a boundary while showing respect for a student's question.

  • "I understand how my feedback might have come across."

    This is a non-defensive way for a teacher to accept feedback from a student or a class.

  • "I'm not sure, but let's look it up together."

    This keeps the teacher teachable and shows that it's okay not to have all the answers.

  • "That wasn't my intention, but I see how my words affected you."

    This models owning impact, not just intent, in a classroom setting.

  • "Thanks for pointing that out..."

    This demonstrates accountability and teaches students that mistakes are opportunities for learning.

  • "If I'm incorrect about that, I want to understand why."

    This makes space for teacher growth without defensiveness and models intellectual humility.

De-escalating & Validating Feelings

  • "You might be on to something there."

    This disarms tension in a discussion and encourages further exploration.

  • "It's completely fine not to know the answer..."

    This shows emotional security and reduces the pressure to be perfect.

  • "Let's take a break from this..."

    This teaches emotional regulation and guides a return to clarity.

  • "You don't have to explain why..."

    This offers emotional safety and respects a student's privacy.

  • "Let's take a breath..."

    This creates a calm space in a tense conversation or moment.
